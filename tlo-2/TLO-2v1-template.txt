\vspace{12 pt}

A \VAR{data.name} nucleus (\VAR{data.protons} protons and \VAR{data.neutrons} neutrons, with mass = $\dfrac{\VAR{data.mNum}\times10^{-3}\mathrm{kg/mole}}{6\times10^{23} \mathrm{atoms/mole}}$) accelerates to the right due to electric forces, and the initial magnitude of the acceleration is \VAR{data.aVal}x10$^{13}$ meters per second per second.

\vspace{11 pt}

(a) What is the direction of the electric field that acts on the lithium nucleus?

%% if ans
The correct direction is \VAR{ans.a}. Since the nucleus as a positive charge, the accleration, force, and field, are all in the same direction.
%% endif

\vspace{1 in}

(b) What is the magnitude of the electric field that acts on the \VAR{data.name} nucleus?  Be quantitative (that is, give a number).  Explain briefly.

%%if ans
The magnitude of the electric field is \VAR{ans.E} N/C.

$$ E = \frac{F}{q} = \frac{m a}{q_e} = \frac{\VAR{data.mStr}{\rm kg} \times \VAR{data.aStr} {\rm m/s^2}}{\VAR{data.qStr}{\rm C}} $$

%%endif
